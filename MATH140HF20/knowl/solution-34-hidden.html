To identify the critical points we take the derivative of \(g\)

\begin{equation*} g'(x) = 3x^2-12 = 3(x^2-4)=3(x-2)(x+2) \end{equation*}

The derivative is zero at \(x=-2\) and \(x=2\text{.}\) To use the second derivative test we take the second derivative.

\begin{equation*} g''(x) = 6x \end{equation*}

We then plug in our critical points

\begin{equation*} g''(-2)=-12\quad g''(2)=12 \end{equation*}

and see there is a local max at \(x=-2\) and a local min at \(x=2\text{.}\)
