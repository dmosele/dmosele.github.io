Section 2.3 Section 2.4 - Interpreting Derivatives

Worksheet 2.3.1 Worksheet

1.
On a hot summer day, Bob takes a carton of ice cream out of a 10\(^\circ\)F freezer and forgets about it. Suppose room temperature is \(75^\circ\)F.

  1. Sketch a graph of temperature \(f(t)\) of the ice cream as a function of time, where \(t\) is time in hours.

  2. What are the units of \(f'(t)\text{?}\)
  3. Is the derivative positive or negative?
  4. Interpret the statement \(f'(4)=3\) in the context of the problem.
Solution
  2. The units are \(^\circ\)F per hour (always output per input)
  3. The derivative is positive as the temperature is increasing
  4. After 4 hours, the temperature of the ice cream is increasing at a rate of 3\(^\circ\)F per hour.
