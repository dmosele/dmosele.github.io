You can evaluate this graphically, but to me the best tactic is to divide the numerator and denominator by the largest power of \(x\text{.}\) In this case that is \(x^2\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to \infty} \frac{4x+5}{6x^2+7x+8} = \lim_{x\to \infty} \frac{\frac4x+\frac5{x^2}}{6+\frac7x+\frac8{x^2}} = 0 \end{equation*}
Note that everything in the numerator goes to zero, so the fraction as a whole goes to zero (as the denominator is nonzero).
