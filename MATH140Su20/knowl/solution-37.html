Solution 6.3.1.2.1.
The general antiderivative looks like
\begin{equation*} F(x) = 2x+2x^2+\frac{5x^3}{3}+C \end{equation*}
and we want to find the one with \(F(0)=0\text{.}\) Note that plugging 0 into the above formula yields \(F(0)=C\text{,}\) hence we need \(C=0\text{.}\) Our final answer is
\begin{equation*} F(x) = 2x+2x^2+\frac{5x^3}{3} \text{.} \end{equation*}
