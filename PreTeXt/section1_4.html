Section 1.4 Formulas for Linear Functions

Worksheet 1.4.1 Worksheet

1.

Compute a formula for the linear function graphed below.

Solution
The slope is
\begin{equation*} \frac{22-13}{5-2} = \frac{9}{3} = 3 \end{equation*}
Using the point-slope form we get
\begin{equation*} y-13 = 3(x-2) \quad \Rightarrow \quad y=3x+7 \end{equation*}
2.
  1. Find the equation of a line that is parallel to \(y=4x-2\) that passes through the point \((2,5)\text{.}\)
  2. Find the equation of a line that is perpendicular to \(y=3x+7\) that passes through the point \((3,5)\text{.}\)
Solution
  1. Since the lines are parallel, the new slope is \(4\text{.}\) Using the point-slope form we get
    \begin{equation*} y-5=4(x-2) \quad \Rightarrow \quad y=4x-3 \end{equation*}
  2. Since the lines are perpendicular, the new slope is \(\frac{-1}{3}\text{.}\) Using the point-slope form we get
    \begin{equation*} y-5=\frac{-1}{3}(x-3) \quad \Rightarrow \quad y=\frac{-1}{3}x+6 \end{equation*}
3.
The profit from selling widgets at a widget factory is given by the following table
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{l|c|c|c|c|c} w \text{ (number of widgets)} & 5 & 10 & 20 & 50 & 100 \\ \hline P \text{ (profit in dollars)} & 1 & 7 & 19 & 55 & 115 \end{array} \end{equation*}
  1. Assuming that profit is linear, find a formula for profit as a function of number of widgets sold.
  2. Why do you think that the vertical intercept (your \(b\) value) is negative?
Solution
  1. We can use any two points here, so I'll use \((5,1)\) and \((10,7)\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} m = \frac{7-1}{10-5}=\frac 65 = 1.2 \end{equation*}
    To compute the equation of the line, we use the point-slope form
    \begin{equation*} y-1 = 1.2(x-5) \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = 1.2x-5 \end{equation*}
  2. When first making a product, there will be initial costs that are only recovered once you start selling your product. Thus, profit will start out negative.
