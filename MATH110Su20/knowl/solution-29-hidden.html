As the function is of the form \(y=a\cdot b^x\text{,}\) we see that

\begin{equation*} 24=ab^4 \text{ and } 6=ab^1 \end{equation*}

Dividing the two equations yields

\begin{equation*} \frac{24=ab^4}{6=ab^1} \quad \Rightarrow \quad 4 = b^3 \quad \Rightarrow \quad b = \sqrt[3]{4} \end{equation*}

Now that we know \(b\text{,}\) we can find \(a\) by using one of the above equations

\begin{equation*} 6=a\sqrt[3]{4}^1 \Rightarrow a = \frac{6}{\sqrt[3]{4}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y=\frac{6}{\sqrt[3]{4}}(\sqrt[3]{4})^x \end{equation*}
