Item 5.2.1.2.b.

We now set \(P\) equal to 22000 and solve for \(t\)

\begin{equation*} 22000=11000\left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)^t \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2 = \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)^t \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \ln(2) = t \cdot \ln \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} t = \frac{\ln(2)}{\ln \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)} \approx 12.45 \text{ years} \end{equation*}
