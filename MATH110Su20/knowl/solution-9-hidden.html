1. Since the lines are parallel, the new slope is \(4\text{.}\) Using the point-slope form we get
    \begin{equation*} y-5=4(x-2) \quad \Rightarrow \quad y=4x-3 \end{equation*}

    \begin{equation*} y-5=\frac{-1}{3}(x-3) \quad \Rightarrow \quad y=\frac{-1}{3}x+6 \end{equation*}
