Section 1.2 Rate of Change

Worksheet 1.2.1 Worksheet

1.

Let \(f(x) = x^2-3x+7\text{.}\) Compute the average rate of change from \(x=1\) to \(x=4\text{.}\) Visualize the average rate of change using the line between the two points \((1,f(1))\) and \((4,f(4))\text{.}\)

Hint
How many units does the line segment go up as you move one unit to the right?
Answer
\(2\)
Solution
The average rate of change is \(\frac{f(4)-f(1)}{4-1} = \frac{11-5}{3}=2 \text{.}\) We can see it from the line segment above. As we go to the right one unit, we go up 2 units.
2.

The total number of Netflix subscribers 1  in a given year between 2010 and 2015 is given by the table below.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c} \text{Year} & 2010 & 2011 & 2012 & 2013 & 2014 & 2015 \\ \hline \text{Subscribers (in millions)} & 20.01 & 23.53 & 33.27 & 44.35 & 57.39 & 74.76 \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
  1. Determine the rate of change of the number of Netflix subscribers between 2010 and 2013.
  2. Determine the rate of change of the number of Netflix subscribers between 2013 and 2015, and compare to you answer from part (a). What do you think this says about Netflix's growth?
https://www.businessinsider.com/netflix-subscribers-chart-2017-1
Answer
  1. \(\displaystyle 8.11\overline{3} \text{ million subscribers per year}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 15.205 \text{ million subscribers per year}\)
Solution
  1. Using the rate of change formula we get
    \begin{equation*} RoC_{2010\leq x \leq 2013} = \frac{44.35-20.01}{2013-2010} = \frac{24.34}{3} = 8.11\overline{3} \text{ million subscribers per year} \end{equation*}
  2. Using the rate of change formula we get
    \begin{equation*} RoC_{2013\leq x \leq 2015} = \frac{74.76-44.35}{2015-2013} = \frac{30.41}{2} = 15.205 \text{ million subscribers per year} \end{equation*}
    Comparing to part (a) we see that we get a larger result and hence the average number of subscriptions looks to be increasing faster in the second time interval.
