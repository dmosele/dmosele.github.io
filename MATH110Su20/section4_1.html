Section 4.1 Exponential Functions #1

Worksheet 4.1.1 Worksheet

1.
The population of Forks, Washington was 3060 in the year 1980 and grew by \(3.4\%\) each year.
  1. How many people lived in Forks in 1981? 1982?
  2. Write a formula for population \(P\) as a function of time \(t\) since 1980.
  3. What is predicted for the population in the year 2013?
Solution

  1. To get the number of people in Forks in 1981 and 1982 we increase by 3.4% once and twice respectively.

    \begin{equation*} \text{Pop in 1981} = 3060\cdot 1.034 \approx 3164 \text{ people} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{Pop in 1982} = 3060\cdot 1.034^2 \approx 3272 \text{ people} \end{equation*}
  2. \(\displaystyle P = f(t) = 3060 (1.034)^t \)
  3. \(\displaystyle f(33) = 3060(1.034)^{33}\approx 9224\text{ people} \)
2.
The radioactive isotope Radon-222 decays at a rate of \(16.7\%\) per day. 1 
  1. Write a function giving the mass \(M\) of a 500 gram sample after a time \(t\) in days.
  2. How much will we have remaining after 3 days?

  3. (hard) When will we only have half of our initial sample remaining? (Hint: Look at the graph of your exponential function, and the graph of \(y=250\text{.}\))

Solution

  1. Since the quantity decreases by \(16.7\%\) each day, there is \(83.3\%\) remaining.

    \begin{equation*} M = g(t) = 500(.833)^t \end{equation*}
  2. \(\displaystyle g(3) = 500(.833)^3 \approx 289.005 \text{ grams}\)

  3. We see that the curves intersect when \(t=3.793\text{.}\)
