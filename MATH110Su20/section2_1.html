Section 2.1 Input and Output

Worksheet 2.1.1 Worksheet

1.
Let \(f(x) = x^2-9\text{.}\)
  1. Compute \(f(0)\text{.}\)
  2. Solve \(f(x) = 0\text{.}\)
  3. Compute \(f(\blacksquare)\text{.}\)
  4. Compute and simplify \(f(x+\pi)\text{.}\)
Solution
  1. \(f(0)=0^2-9=-9\text{.}\)
  2. \begin{equation*} x^2-9= 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x^2=0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = \pm \sqrt{9} = \pm 3 \text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. \(f(\blacksquare)=\blacksquare^2-9\text{.}\)
  4. \(f(x+\pi)=(x+\pi)^2-9 = x^2+2\pi x+\pi^2-9\text{.}\)
2.
Marvin the Martian has height given by \(h(t) = 13+\frac{t^2}{2}\) where \(t\) is the number of years since Marvin was born and height is in inches.
  1. How tall was Marvin at birth (time zero)?
  2. When will Marvin be 21 inches tall?
  3. What is the meaning of the quantity \(h(5)\text{?}\) What is its units?
Solution
  1. \(h(0)=13+\frac{0^2}{2}=13\) inches tall
  2. We solve \(21=13+\frac{t^2}{2}\) which is equivalent to \(8=\frac{t^2}{2}\) or \(16=t^2\text{.}\) This is true when \(t=\pm 4\text{,}\) but only \(t=4\) years makes sense in this context.
  3. \(h(5)=13+\frac{5^2}{2} = 25.5\) inches and it represents Marvin's height at 5 years old.
3.
Answer the following questions about the function \(f(x)\) using its graph below.

  1. Fill in the following table
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c} x & -3 & -2 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ \hline f(x)& \hspace{.2in} & \hspace{.2in} & \hspace{.2in} & \hspace{.2in} & \hspace{.2in} & \hspace{.2in} \end{array} \end{equation*}
  2. Evaluate \(f(0)-f(-2)\)
  3. Evaluate \(3f(0)\)
Solution
  1. \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c} x & -3 & -2 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ \hline f(x)& 0& 9 & 8 & 3 & 0 & 5 \end{array} \end{equation*}

  2. \begin{equation*} f(0)-f(-2) = 3-9=-6 \end{equation*}
  3. \begin{equation*} 3f(0) = 3\cdot 3=9 \end{equation*}
