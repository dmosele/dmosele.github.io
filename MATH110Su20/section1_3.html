Section 1.3 Linear Functions

Worksheet 1.3.1 Worksheet

1.
After being on Instagram for a while, Sam started keeping track of her followers. Let N represent the number of followers she has on Instagram at time \(t\) in months since she started keeping track.
\begin{equation*} \begin{array} { l | c | c | c | c | c } t \text{ (months)} & 0 & 1 & 3 & 7 & 10 \\ \hline N \text{ (number of followers)} & 70 & 95 & 145 & 245 & 320 \end{array} \end{equation*}
  1. Could this function be linear? (Check the rate of change over each interval.)
  2. What is the slope of this linear function?
  3. What does the slope mean in real world terms?
  4. Write a formula for \(N\) as a function of \(t\text{.}\) (What is the vertical intercept?)
  5. What does your vertical intercept represent in real world terms?
Solution

  1. Using the rate of change formula we get

    \begin{equation*} RoC_{0\leq t \leq 1} = \frac{95-70}{1-0} = 25 \text{ followers per month} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} RoC_{1\leq t \leq 3} = \frac{145-95}{3-1} = 25 \text{ followers per month} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} RoC_{3\leq t \leq 7} = \frac{245-145}{7-3} = 25 \text{ followers per month} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} RoC_{7\leq t \leq 10} = \frac{320-245}{10-7} = 25 \text{ followers per month} \end{equation*}

    From this data, the function looks linear as the rate of change is constant over every interval.

  2. The slope is 25 as the slope is the constant rate of change of a linear function.
  3. The slope represents the change in followers per month. That is, the number of followers increases by 25 per month.
  4. \(\displaystyle N= 70+25t \)
  5. The vertical intercept here is 70 which represents the initial number of followers when Sam started keeping track.
2.
The enrollment at JU is given by the following graph of a linear function where \(S\) represents the number of students, and \(t\) represents the number of years since 2000.

  1. Using the two given points on the graph, determine the rate of change (slope) of the linear function.
  2. How many students were there in the year 2000?
  3. Determine a formula for \(S\) as a function of \(t\)
Solution

  1. The slope is

    \begin{equation*} m = \frac{2744-2636}{4-1} = 36. \end{equation*}
  2. If the number of students increases by 36 each year, then going back one year from 2001 to 2000 should result in a decrease in 36 from the 2636 students that were at JU in 2000. Hence, there were 2600 students in 2000.

  3. Using the two pieces of data above

    \begin{equation*} S = 2600+36t \end{equation*}
