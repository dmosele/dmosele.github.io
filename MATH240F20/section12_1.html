Section 1.1 Section 12.1 - Functions of Two Variables

Worksheet 1.1.1 Worksheet

1.

What is the distance between the points \((1,3,4)\) and \((9,0,4)\text{?}\)

Solution

The distance is

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{(1-9)^2+(3-0)^2+(4-4)^2}=\sqrt{64+9+0}=\sqrt{73} \end{equation*}
2.

A bank launches a new savings account with an annual interest rate of \(5\%\text{.}\) Find a formula for the function of two varialbes giving the account balance \(t\) years after an initial investment of \(P\) dollars.

Solution

The account balance, a function of two variables, is given by

\begin{equation*} f(t,P)=P(1.05)^t. \end{equation*}
3.

Come up with a formula for a cylinder of radius 2 centered on the \(x\)-axis.

Solution

The formula shouldn't involve \(x\) because the \(x\)-coordinates have no restrictions. Instead, we should think about the circle created in the \(yz\)-plane which has formula \(y^2+z^2=4\text{.}\)
