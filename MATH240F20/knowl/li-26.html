Item 7.1.1.1.b.
\(\mathrm{grad}(g) = 2x\vec i + 2y \vec j\) so we are solving the system of equations
\begin{equation*} 2x = 2x\lambda \quad \text{and} \quad 4y=2y\lambda \end{equation*}
This is a strange one because solving both equations for \(\lambda\) yields that \(\lambda=1\) and \(\lambda =2\text{.}\) The one thing we ignored here was the possibilites that \(x=0\) or \(y=0\text{.}\) If \(x=0\text{,}\) then substituting back into the constraint equation yields \(0^2+y^2=4\) which means that \(y=\pm 2\text{.}\) If \(y=0\text{,}\) substituting into the constraint equation yields \(x^2+0^2=4\) which means that \(x=\pm 2\text{.}\) Thus, we get 4 important points out of this
\begin{equation*} (0,-2),(0,2),(2,0),(-2,0) \end{equation*}
