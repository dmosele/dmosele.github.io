1. We start with what we know, namely that \(P=11000b^t\) as the initial quantity is 11000. We then use the fact that \(P\) is 13000 when \(t=3\) to see

    \begin{equation*} 13000=11000b^3 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{13000}{11000} = b^3 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} = b \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} P = 11000\left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)^t \end{equation*}

  2. We now set \(P\) equal to 22000 and solve for \(t\)

    \begin{equation*} 22000=11000\left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)^t \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2 = \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)^t \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \ln(2) = t \cdot \ln \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right) \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} t = \frac{\ln(2)}{\ln \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{13000}{11000}} \right)} \approx 12.45 \text{ years} \end{equation*}
