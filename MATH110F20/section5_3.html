Section 9.2 Section 5.3 - Finishing Up Exponential Functions

Worksheet 9.2.1 Worksheet

1.

The technology company Orange started selling its oPhone in 2015 with sales of 10000 oPhones. By 2018 it sold 500000 oPhones. Model the growth of oPhone sales using an exponential function of the form

\begin{equation*} f(t)= ae^{kt} \end{equation*}
\(t\)Solution

We start with the fact that we know \(f(t)=10000e^{kt}\) as the initial quantity was 10000. Then we use that when \(t=3\text{,}\) the number of phones is 500000.

\begin{equation*} 500000=10000e^{k\cdot 3} \quad \Rightarrow \quad 50 = e^{3k} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \ln(50)=\ln(e^{3k})=3k \quad \Rightarrow \quad k = \frac{\ln(50)}{3} \approx 1.30401 \end{equation*}

Putting it all together, we get

\begin{equation*} f(t) = 10000e^{1.30401t} \end{equation*}
2.
\(f(t)=3(0.5)^t\)\(f(t)=ae^{kt}\)\(k\)Solution

Here, we remember that \(b=e^k\) and since \(b=.5\text{,}\) we can use that to solve for \(k\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} 0.5=e^k \quad \Rightarrow \quad \ln(0.5)=\ln(e^k)=k \end{equation*}

Note that \(\ln(0.5) \approx -0.6931\) so

\begin{equation*} f(t)=3e^{-0.6931t} \end{equation*}
